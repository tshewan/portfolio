Digital health book

Aim

To develop a comprehensive Digital Healthbook application that provides seamless access to patient health records, facilitating better health management and coordination among healthcare providers.

Objectives

  • Centralized Health Records: Provide a central platform for storing and accessing patient health records to streamline healthcare delivery and improve patient outcomes.
  • Real-time Health Data Access: Allow healthcare providers and patients to access health information in real-time, ensuring timely decisions based on the latest data.
  • Automated Alerts: Implement alert systems for patient monitoring and reminders for medication, appointments, and health checks.
  • Enhanced Data Security: Apply strict security protocols and encryption methods to protect sensitive health information against unauthorized access and breaches.

Pain Points

User Accessibility

Users may find it challenging to navigate complex health interfaces, especially older adults or those with limited tech skills, leading to frustration and reduced usage.

Personalization Gaps

The lack of personalized experiences can deter user engagement, as generic interfaces fail to address individual health needs and preferences.

Mobile Optimization

Many digital health applications are not fully optimized for mobile devices, creating barriers for users who primarily access information through smartphones.

Data Overload

Users often feel overwhelmed by the amount of data presented, making it difficult to discern important health information and actionable insights.
